"I hate math." That's how Maya introduced herself in her first Strive class.

She was 10, smart, creative — but completely shut off when numbers showed up. When we dug deeper, we discovered something surprising:

She didn't actually hate math.

She hated *feeling inferior*.

Maya had missed a couple of key concepts during online learning in COVID. So every new topic felt like building a tower on sand — wobbly, frustrating, and bound to collapse.

---

In her first class, her teacher at Strive ditched the worksheet. Instead, they opened a blank canvas and said:

**"Let's code your dream pet. But we'll need some math to make it move."**

Suddenly, math had a purpose.

Equations weren't abstract anymore — they controlled the pet's jump height, speed, and colour changes.

Maya was *laughing*.
*Asking questions.*
*Solving proportions.*
*Multiplying decimals.*

All in the name of making her virtual dragon breathe fire.

---

If your child is struggling with math, it doesn't mean they're not smart.

It just means they haven't experienced math in a way that makes sense to *them* — yet.

At Strive, we teach math through **coding, creativity, and real-world projects** so every child can finally say, *"Now I get it."*
